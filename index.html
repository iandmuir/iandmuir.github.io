About

Ian specializes in data-driven analysis to inform the design of renewable energy financing programs, build impact modeling tools, and drive scalable deployment of off-grid solar systems, green mini-grids, and clean cooking solutions. Previously, he served as the World Bank's Lighting Africa country lead for Rwanda and Madagascar, where he spearheaded initiatives to expand rural electrification, and as a Project Manager for EarthSpark International's mini-grid program in Haiti.

He holds an MA in Chemistry from Trinity College, Dublin, and an MA in International Relations and Economics from Johns Hopkins University's School of Advanced International Studies (SAIS).

When he isn't crunching numbers for the energy transition, Ian is likely adding to his collection of passports. He currently holds three and is actively working on a fourth. Fluent in French and Portuguese, and functional in Spanish, Ian has lived in ten countries across five continents, but currently rests his wandering feet in Washington, D.C., where he lives with his wife and daughter.
